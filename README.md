## How It Works: The Three Main Ideas

### 1️⃣ The Energy Secret: 1 Kilogram Equals 7,700 Calories

This is the foundation. Science tells us that **one kilogram of body weight ≈ 7,700 calories**.

Think of it like this:
- If you eat 2,400 calories today and burn 2,500 calories (in workouts, daily living, etc.)
- You have a 100-calorie *deficit*
- Over 77 days, that 100 × 77 = 7,700 calories = 1 kg of fat lost

So when you weigh yourself a week later and see 0.3 kg lost, you know: **you had a 0.3 × 7,700 = 2,310 calorie deficit over the week**, or about **330 calories per day deficit**.

**In simpler math per day:**
- 7,700 calories ÷ 7 days = 1,100 calories per day
- **Every 1 kg/week of weight loss means a 1,100 calorie/day deficit**
- **Every 1 lb/week means a 500 calorie/day deficit**

This is the magic number that runs everything.

### 2️⃣ Weekly Medians: Filtering Out Side Effects

Your weight bounces around like crazy, but NOT because of fat loss/gain. It's all noise:

**Water fluctuations:**
- Ate salty food yesterday? You're retaining 1-2 kg of water (sodium holds water in your body)
- Drank 4 liters of water today? Temporary weight gain until you pee it out
- Carbs store water (3g of carbs = 1g water retention). Change your carbs? Scale swings wildly
- Low-carb diet? Loses 2-3 kg in week 1, but that's mostly water, not fat

**Hormonal & biological factors:**
- Period/menstrual cycle? Can cause 1-3 kg water retention for days
- Stress and cortisol? Increases water retention
- Less sleep? Body holds onto water
- Exercise? Muscle inflammation from workouts = water retention for 24-48 hours
- Digestion: How much is sitting in your stomach/intestines right now?

**The problem:** If you weigh yourself after a hard workout, you might see +0.5 kg even though you burned 500 calories. That's all inflammation and water.

**The solution: Use a 7-day MEDIAN weight**

Over a full week, all these side effects average out. Water retention happens some days. Water loss happens other days. Inflammation goes down. Hormones cycle. Your digestion normalizes.

**The median is the middle number when you sort from lowest to highest:**

Example: If you weighed yourself Mon-Sun: 75.0, 74.8, 75.2, 74.9, 75.1, 74.7, 75.3

**Sorted:** 74.7, 74.8, 74.9, **75.0**, 75.1, 75.2, 75.3

**The median is 75.0** (the middle one).

**Here's the magic:** Over 7 days, water fluctuations cancel out. Salt bloat cancels out. Inflammation subsides. Hormone swings smooth out. 

**What's left?** Actual fat loss or gain.

So when the median goes from 75.5 kg to 75.0 kg (a 0.5 kg drop over the week), you can be confident that's **real fat loss**, not water noise.

**Why median instead of average?** Because if one day you're bloated (76 kg), an average would drag up the whole week. The median ignores extreme outliers and gives you the true middle ground where you actually spend most days.

**The bottom line:** The 0.5 kg median drop ≈ 0.5 kg of real fat + water loss (mostly fat). This is why the math works so well—you're not chasing daily fluctuations. You're tracking real metabolic change.

### 3️⃣ Comparing Two Weeks: The Adjustment Logic

Now here's where the magic happens. Let's say:

**Previous week's median:** 75.5 kg  
**Current week's median:** 75.0 kg  
**Your calorie intake:** 2,400 kcal/day  
**Your weekly goal:** Lose 0.5 kg/week

**What happened:**
- You actually lost: 75.5 - 75.0 = 0.5 kg ✅ **Perfect!**
- You hit your goal exactly
- **Adjustment needed:** 0 calories

**But here's the realistic scenario:**

**Previous week's median:** 75.5 kg  
**Current week's median:** 75.2 kg  
**Your calorie intake:** 2,400 kcal/day  
**Your weekly goal:** Lose 0.5 kg/week

**What happened:**
- You actually lost: 75.5 - 75.2 = 0.3 kg ❌ **Not enough!**
- You wanted: 0.5 kg
- **You're short by:** 0.3 - 0.5 = -0.2 kg
- **In calories:** -0.2 kg × 1,100 kcal/kg = -220 calories/day
- **So you need to eat:** 2,400 - 220 = **2,180 calories/day**

**The thinking:** You weren't in a big enough deficit. By eating 220 fewer calories, you'll create the missing deficit and hit your 0.5 kg/week goal.

## The Three Paths: Loss, Gain, or Maintenance

### Path 1: Weight Loss Goal 🔴

**Your mindset:** "I want to lose X kg per week"

**The calculation:**
```
Deficit needed = (Actual loss - Your goal) × 1,100 kcal/day

New calorie target = Current calories - Deficit needed
```

**Real example:**
- Goal: Lose 1 lb (0.45 kg) per week
- You ate 2,400 calories
- You actually lost only 0.2 kg
- **Gap:** 0.2 - 0.45 = -0.25 kg
- **Adjustment:** -0.25 × 1,100 = -275 calories/day
- **New target:** 2,400 - 275 = **2,125 calories/day**

**Coach message:** "You're losing slower than planned. Cut 275 calories to hit your target. This is sustainable and healthy."

---

### Path 2: Weight Gain Goal 🟢

**Your mindset:** "I want to gain X kg per week (muscle building)"

**The calculation:**
```
Surplus deficit = (Actual gain - Your goal) × 1,100 kcal/day

New calorie target = Current calories - Surplus deficit
```

Wait, why is it backwards? Because if you're not gaining enough, you need to ADD calories, not subtract.

**Real example:**
- Goal: Gain 0.5 kg per week
- You ate 2,800 calories
- You only gained 0.2 kg
- **Gap:** 0.2 - 0.5 = -0.3 kg (not enough gain)
- **Adjustment:** -(-0.3) × 1,100 = +330 calories/day (need to ADD)
- **New target:** 2,800 + 330 = **3,130 calories/day**

**Coach message:** "You're gaining slower than planned. Eat 330 more calories to hit your target. This supports clean muscle growth."

---

### Path 3: Maintenance Mode ⚪

**Your mindset:** "My goal weight is my current weight. I just want to stabilize."

**The calculation (if already there):**
```
If weight difference < 50g: No change needed, you're at maintenance!
```

**The calculation (if not there yet):**
```
Default rate = Move toward goal at 0.25 kg/week (slowly)
OR manually adjust using the rate slider
```

**Real example:**
- Goal weight: 75 kg
- Current weight: 74.8 kg
- You're 0.2 kg below goal (losing slightly)
- Tool suggests: Gain 0.25 kg/week to reach it
- **Default adjustment:** 0.25 × 1,100 = +275 calories/day
- You can adjust this to be slower or faster

**Coach message:** "You're drifting slightly below your goal. Add a small surplus to stabilize. You can fine-tune the weekly rate."

## The Bonus Calculation: Estimated TDEE

**TDEE = Total Daily Energy Expenditure** (how many calories your body actually burns)

Here's where this tool gets REALLY powerful. Once you enter your current calories and the tool sees your weight change, it can estimate your real TDEE.

**The thinking:**
```
If you reported eating 2,400 calories and lost 0.3 kg:
- 0.3 kg loss = 330 calorie/day deficit
- Your TDEE must be: 2,400 + 330 = 2,730 kcal/day

Because:
- You ate: 2,400 calories
- Gap: -330 calories (deficit)
- Actual burn: 2,400 + 330 = 2,730 calories
```

**Why this is so accurate:**
- Generic TDEE formulas guess based on genders/ages/heights
- **This uses your actual weight change** (which is smoothed by the median, so it's real fat loss, not water noise)
- Real fat loss = real energy deficit = real metabolism revealed
- It gets more accurate over weeks of data

**Real example with the truth:**
- You ate 2,400 calories/day
- Your median weight dropped 0.5 kg over the week
- That 0.5 kg is real fat (median removed water fluctuation noise)
- So your actual deficit was: 0.5 kg × 1,100 kcal/kg = 550 kcal/day
- Therefore: Your TDEE = 2,400 + 550 = **2,950 kcal/day**

This is *your* metabolism, not a formula's guess.

**Real coaching use:**
> "Your estimated TDEE is 2,730 calories. At 2,400 calories, you're creating a 330 calorie/day deficit, which should lose you about 0.3 kg per week. Looks accurate!"

## The Critical Insight: Median Weight Change = Real Fat Loss

This is the secret sauce that makes the entire tool work:

**When you compare two weekly medians, the difference is REAL metabolic change.**

Over a single day, weight fluctuates from:

**Over 7 days, these all cancel out.** You're sodium-loaded some days, depleted others. You exercise and retain water, then it goes away. Your hormones cycle through. Bowel movements happen.

**The median removes all this noise because it's the middle ground.**

### The Math Truth

```
Median Weight Change ≈ Fat + Water from Diet ≈ Real Energy Deficit

Not:   Water retention from salt
```

## The Safety Guards: Why The Tool Warns You

### ⚠️ "Below 1,200 Calories"

The tool screams at you if your target drops below 1,200 calories per day because:
- This is the absolute minimum for healthy dieting
- Below this = muscle loss, fatigue, metabolism damage
- The tool is telling you: "Something is wrong with your numbers"

**What to do:** Use a smaller weekly goal (like 0.5 kg instead of 1 kg per week).

### ⚠️ "Extreme Adjustment (>1,500 calories)"

If the tool calculates you need to change calories by more than 1,500 per day:
- This often means you made a unit mistake (entered lbs as kg)
- Or your weekly goal is unrealistic
- The tool is telling you: "This seems wrong, double-check"

**What to do:** Verify your goal isn't too aggressive. A 1.5 kg/week loss is extreme and unsustainable.

## The Data: Where Is Everything Stored?

Everything lives in your **browser's localStorage** (local saving):
- Your weight entries
- Your calculation history
- Your preferences (kg vs lbs)
- Your theme (dark vs light mode)

**Why this way?**
- ✅ Your data stays 100% private
- ✅ Works offline
- ✅ No server needed
- ✅ Instantly available

**The downside:**
- If you clear browser history/cookies, it wipes data
- Limited to ~5 MB (enough for years of data)

**To backup:** Right-click → Inspect → Application → localStorage → Copy the data

## Common Mistakes & How To Fix Them

### ❌ "I entered my weight in lbs but the tool thinks it's kg"

**The problem:** You said 165 lbs, but the tool treats it as 165 kg (way too heavy).

**The fix:** Use the unit toggle at the top. Switch to lbs and re-enter your weight. The tool will convert it correctly.

**How it works:** All math happens in kg internally. The toggle just changes how numbers display.

### ❌ "My adjustment is huge (like 1,000+ calories)"

**The problem:** Either a mistake in your numbers or your goal is unrealistic.

**The fix:** Check:
1. Are units correct? (Did you mean to enter 0.5 kg, not 5 kg?)
2. Is your weekly goal realistic? (1 kg/week is aggressive; 0.5 kg is standard)
3. Are your medians entered correctly?

### ❌ "My weight keeps jumping around by 2 kg"

**The problem:** You're looking at daily weight, not weekly median.

**The fix:** Enter weight for 7 days, then look at the weekly median. It'll be stable. One day doesn't matter.

### ❌ "I deleted my data by accident"

**The problem:** You cleared browser cache or used incognito mode.

**The fix:** Unfortunately, localStorage data is gone. But if you have backups, you can restore by pasting into developer tools. Going forward, export/backup regularly.
