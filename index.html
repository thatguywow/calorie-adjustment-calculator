How to Use the Calculator

This tool adjusts your daily calorie intake based on weekly weight trends, then stores every result in your browser for later review.

Weekly Median Weight

  • Weigh yourself at the same time each day for a week.
  • Log each weigh-in in the Weight Log tab.
  • The app groups entries into 7-day blocks and calculates the true median.

Using the Calculator

  • Select your goal type.
  • Enter the previous and current weekly medians.
  • Set your weekly target rate for loss or gain.
  • Enter your current calories and calculate the update.

Coaching Mode

  • Shows a practical next step based on your goal and trend.
  • Highlights aggressive cuts that may be too hard to sustain.
  • Estimates your TDEE from the trend you entered.

How to Use the Weight Log

The Weight Log keeps every entry in browser storage so you can revisit any date later.

Saving Entries

  • Select a date and enter your weight.
  • Use the same date again to update that entry.
  • All entries stay saved in localStorage unless you delete them.

Looking Back

  • Pick any saved date in the history lookup field.
  • See that exact weight, the weekly median, and the previous week median.
  • Copy either median into the calculator with one click.

Graphs

  • The weight chart shows daily weights and weekly medians.
  • The calorie chart keeps a history of your past adjustments.

How the Math Works

This tool uses the scientifically accepted energy equivalent of body-fat change:

  • 1 kg ≈ 7700 kcal
  • 1 lb ≈ 3500 kcal

Core formula (loss/gain):
Daily adjustment = (Actual weekly median change − Your target weekly change) × daily factor

Daily factor = 1100 kcal (kg) or 500 kcal (lbs).

TDEE estimate = Current calories − (Current median − Previous median) × daily factor

All math runs internally in kilograms for accuracy. Your chosen unit only affects display and input.

This is why the calculator is far more accurate than generic TDEE formulas — it reacts to your real weekly trend.
